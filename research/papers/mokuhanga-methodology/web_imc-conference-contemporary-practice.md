## International Mokuhanga Association (IMA)

The International Mokuhanga Association organizes triennial conferences (IMC) bringing together practitioners, academics, educators, gallerists, and collectors. The conferences have been held in Hawaii (2011, 2017), Nara (2021, online), and Echizen (2024). The next conference is Lawrence, Kansas (2027).

## IMC 2021: Sumi-Fusion (Nara/Online)

The 2021 conference included 40 paper presentations organized under themes: Contemporary Mokuhanga, Mokuhanga History, Philosophy & Society, and Bridge-Building Educational Mokuhanga Practice. Papers directly relevant to the chuck-mcp project include those addressing color introduction in multi-block work and technique documentation, though no paper specifically focused on Chuck Close or Pace Editions production was identified in the available schedule.

Notable participants: Paul Binnie ("The Influence of the Past"), Karen Kunc ("Picture This: Illusion and Action in Mokuhanga & Manga"), Ruth Pelzer-Montada ("Mokuhanga Unlimited: The Context of Contemporary Print Media").

## IMC 2024: Echizen Demonstrations

The 2024 Echizen conference included demonstrations on how to divide a design into color blocks — a direct methodological topic for the chuck-mcp planning question. Echizen is itself a significant location: it is one of Japan's traditional washi-producing regions and home to paper mills producing washi for fine art printing.

## Kurosaki Akira's IMC Legacy

Kurosaki Akira (1937–2019), Shibata's teacher, was a foundational figure in contemporary mokuhanga and had strong connections to IMA. His documented practice — 8 blocks, 15 colors, 70 passes for his "Red Darkness" series — establishes the quantitative precedent for Shibata's more extreme production ratios. Kurosaki was the first to demonstrate (in the 1960s–1970s) that contemporary fine art could push mokuhanga into production scales and formal complexities far beyond traditional ukiyo-e.

## Relevant Practitioner Resources

The IMA's Mokuhanga School (mokuhanga-school.jp) produces workshop manuals including the "Mokuhanga Fundamentals" online workshop manual (a PDF that was fetched during this research — binary content, not text-extractable via WebFetch). The manual covers pigment preparation, nori paste, baren use, kento, bokashi — the full technical sequence. It is the most comprehensive free English-language technical resource available online.

## Significance for Chuck-MCP

The IMC ecosystem demonstrates that mokuhanga at production scale is an active research subject with living expert communities. The 2024 Echizen demonstrations on color block separation are directly applicable to the chuck-mcp planning problem. Future research should contact IMA directly (mokuhanga.org) for access to conference paper archives.
