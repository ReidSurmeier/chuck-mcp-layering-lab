## Rebecca Salter, "Japanese Woodblock Printing" (2001/2005)

**Publisher**: A&C Black / University of Hawai'i Press  
**Anna's Archive**: MD5 `bcb210ef60b44caba138bced4db5e78f` (24.9MB PDF) or `c9adbf1e3be6c57ab159730fd549e4c0` (11.8MB, 2005 edition)

Salter is a British artist who studied at Kyoto City University of the Arts and spent six years in Japan learning traditional mokuhanga from Professor Akira Kurosaki (the same teacher as Shibata, Kyoto Seika University). Her book is the most rigorous English-language technique manual for traditional mokuhanga from a practitioner who underwent formal Japanese training.

**Key content documented via secondary sources and reviews**:
- Three elements of quality identified: washi paper (dampened for printing), kento registration, and baren technique — in that order
- Full instructions for kento carving, including the L-notch geometry
- Water-based color preparation: historical natural pigments (mineral and organic) vs. contemporary gouache use
- Complete baren preparation and use (including how to make a traditional hon-baren)
- Paper dampening protocols and the damp-pack method
- Bokashi gradation technique
- Traditional tool types: baren varieties, hashigiri brushes, charging brushes

Salter is among the few Western practitioners to have documented that traditional pigments were "natural (beautiful but very fragile)" while acknowledging that "most printers now use gouache" — establishing the continuity between historical and contemporary material practice.

## April Vollmer, "Japanese Woodblock Print Workshop" (2015)

**Publisher**: Watson-Guptill / Crown Publishing  
**Anna's Archive**: MD5 `33b961fffee2de3d08b3de7d9aaa1f2f` (137.4MB EPUB)

Vollmer is an American practitioner who has collaborated with Japanese master printers and teaches mokuhanga widely in the US. Her book is aimed at contemporary artists and explicitly covers both traditional technique and contemporary adaptation, including:
- Multi-block registration using kento
- Bokashi gradation and its variants
- Contemporary pigment options
- Color mixing and overprint prediction
- Reduction printing basics

Vollmer co-organized the Printopolis 2010 demonstrations documenting "how to repeatedly print multiple blocks to illustrate the process of creating an image larger and more complex than can be made from straightforward printing from a single set of blocks" — directly relevant to the chuck-mcp scale problem.

## Comparison

Salter provides the rigorous traditional foundation; Vollmer bridges to contemporary practice and American studio contexts. Together they constitute the essential technical library for understanding the Shibata-type production.

Salter's book is the correct primary reference for:
- Historical accuracy on tool construction
- Pigment lineage (mineral → synthetic)
- Japanese terminology

Vollmer's book is the correct primary reference for:
- Contemporary material substitutions
- Multi-block workflow for non-ukiyo-e imagery
- Practical studio adaptations

Both are in Anna's Archive and should be acquired.
